Optical Tweezers

THe Bustamante Group

January 2014 - August 2015

This research used instruments called Optical Tweezers for studying the mechanical properties of force-producing enzymes like RNA polymerase in real-time at the single molecule level. These optical tweezers use lasers and the momentum carried by light to trap nano-particles. DNA/RNA pieces are attached to these nano-particles. Using optical tweezers, the motion of the nano-partical can be controlled. As a result, we can join pieces of DNA/RNA, stretch them, calculate the force at which they break, and study the polymerase activity.

For this project, I programmed a system to organize experimental data obtained from Optical Tweezers using Matlab.

I was in charge of using, maintaining, and re-building parts of a Mini Dual Trap Optical Tweezer (shown in the picture below). I also taught the design, mechanism, and functions of this tweezer to visiting scholars.
