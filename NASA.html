The ULTRA Spine

NASA Ames + B.E.S.T Labs

January 2015 - Present

The Underactuated Lightweight Tensegrity Robotic Assistive Spine (ULTRA Spine) project is an ongoing effort to create a compliant, cable-driven, 3-degree-of-freedom, underactuated tensegrity core for quadruped robots. NASA hopes to use this tensegrity spine as a backbone so that their quadruped robots can climb steps, walk over uneven surfaces, and take sharper turns. This will help them explore areas in space that they have not been able to reach before.

I have worked on the design ideation, prototyping, and testing for this robot. Below are some images of my work. Currently, I am publishing a paper, working on force-feedback and impedence control simulations for the spine, and helping with futher design iterations.
